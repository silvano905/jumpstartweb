How to jump start a car

1) Locate the battery

In many cars, the battery sits in the engine compartment under the hood. However, it may also be located in the trunk or under the rear seat of your car. Even when a car battery is hidden in a hard-to-find spot, it’s often wired to the engine compartment to support jump-starting conveniently from under the hood.While it’s extremely rare, batteries can rupture and leak acid, and connecting jumper cables incorrectly can create sparks or damage your electrical components. For this reason, eye and hand protection is very important. In the case of a battery that’s been dead for a long time, it’s best to call professional assistance due to the added risk of complications.

2) Get jumper cables

To jump start a dead car battery from another vehicle identify the positive terminal or connection point under the hood (usually noted by a red cover and a “+” symbol). The best way to know you have found the positive terminal is to look for the positive “+” embossed on the actual battery. Clip one of the positive red clamps to the positive terminal.Next connect the other red clamp to the positive terminal of the car providing the jump, followed by the negative black clamp to the negative terminal of the car providing the jump. Ensure a grounded connection and avoid damaging the battery, attach the final negative clamp to a sizable metallic component of the engine. Don't clamp to thin sheet metal, painted surfaces, or fluid-bearing tubes. Avoid areas around the belts of the engine and the cooling fans.

3) Try starting the car

After letting it run for a few minutes, attempt to start the car with the dead battery. The car may crank slowly, but the goal is to get the engine to start. If the car does not start, check all your connections, adjusting the clamps as needed to ensure full contact, and restart the engine of the car providing the jump. If the car still does not start, leave the cables connected and let the running vehicle charge the dead battery for 5 to 10 minutes before trying to start the dead car again. Once the car with the dead battery is running, disconnect each connection, starting with the negative clamp on the car that provided the jump first. Then, disconnect the negative clamp from the car that received the jump, followed by the positive cables in whichever sequence is most convenient. It’s a good idea to keep the battery in use for 30 minutes before turning off the engine in order to ensure the charge holds. If the battery is nearing the end of its life , it may need to be replaced by a mechanic.
